Cranberry-Orange Chicken

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons Tuscanini Olive Oil
  • 4 to 5 chicken bottoms
  • 1 can Gefen Whole-Berry Cranberry Sauce
  • 1 can Gefen Jellied Cranberry Sauce
  • 1 orange, sliced in half
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • salt, to taste
  • pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large skillet, add olive oil and get the pan very hot. Season the chicken bottoms with salt and pepper and add to the hot pan, skin-side down. Let pan-fry for about 10 minutes and then flip over with tongs. Lower flame.
  2. In a bowl, mix together the two cranberry jelly sauces and squeeze half the orange juice into the bowl. Add the garlic powder and mix well. Pour over all the chicken in the pan. Take the other half of the orange and slice into thin rounds and add to pan. Let everything cook for about 40 minutes, until the chicken is done. Serve over rice.

Note: You can make this on a baking sheet and place in 375 degrees Fahrenheit oven for 55 minutes. Loosely cover pan for the first 30 minutes and uncover till the end.

Tip: You can make this with cutlets as well, but cover the pan to keep the moisture in while baking.

Chicken Nuggets with Vegetable Sesame Sauce

Ingredients

Chicken Nuggets

  • 2 pounds chicken tenders, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 4 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • oil, for frying

Vegetable Sesame Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 eggplant, cubed
  • 1 squash, cubed
  • 1 red pepper, cubed
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 5 cloves garlic, chopped or 5 cubes Gefen Frozen Garlic
  • 1 cup Gefen Duck Sauce
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons Glicks Soy Sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Tuscanini Balsamic Vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon chicken soup consommé
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoons Gefen Sesame Seeds

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, combine cornstarch and spices. Add in the chicken nuggets. Mix and coat each piece well.
  2. In a frying pan heat oil over medium-high heat. Once oil is hot, place each piece of chicken into the pot. Fry until golden brown (approximately seven minutes).
  3. Flip each piece, making sure that all sides are evenly cooked.
  4. Remove chicken from pot and set aside.
  5. Using the same frying pan, heat two tablespoons oil. Add onions and sauté until translucent (approximately seven to eight minutes). Add in cubed eggplant, squash, pepper, and garlic. Sauté for an additional 12 minutes or until both the squash and eggplant are soft.
  6. In a separate bowl combine duck sauce, ketchup, soy sauce, vinegar, hot sauce (if using) and spices. Pour the mixture into the frying pan. Combine with the vegetables. Keep stirring the sauce until it bubbles. Once it bubbles, remove from heat. Add in chicken nuggets and sesame seeds. Enjoy over mashed potatoes or rice.

Note: This dish freezes well.

Pastrami Eggplant Sliders

Ingredients

Pastrami Eggplant Sliders

  • 2-and-1/2-to-3-pound (1.13-to-1.36-kilogram) pastrami roast
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 large Spanish onions, diced
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup Heaven & Earth Ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons Gefen BBQ Sauce
  • 2 tablespoons yellow mustard
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoons Tuscanini Apple Cider Vinegar

Assembly

  • 32 ounces (900 grams) Tuscanini Breaded Eggplant Cutlets (about 16 cutlets)
  • Gefen Garlic Mayo, for drizzling
  • 2–3 jalapeño peppers, seeded and sliced or diced
  • sliced radishes (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 250 degrees Fahrenheit (120 degrees Celsius).
  2. Place pastrami roast into a 9x13-inch (23x33-centimeter) pan filled with about two to three inches (five to seven and a half centimeters) of water. Cover tightly and bake for four to five hours or until extremely tender. Drain the water and shred the pastrami using two forks to pull the meat in until fully shredded.
  3. While pastrami is baking, heat two tablespoons oil in a large frying pan. Add diced onions and sauté over low heat until soft, brown, and caramelized, at least 35–45 minutes. Add brown sugar, ketchup, barbecue sauce, mustard, and vinegar and mix well to combine. Add to shredded pastrami and mix well until combined.
  4. Heat eggplant cutlets according to package instructions, until crisp. To serve, mound about two to three tablespoons of pulled pastrami mixture onto each warm eggplant cutlet. Drizzle with spicy mayo and top with jalapeño peppers and radishes, if desired.

Tip: For a delicious dairy version of the eggplant sliders, try a scoop of mashed potatoes with melted cheese on top of the breaded eggplant.
